Ophiocordyceps unilateralis fungus: The fungus “Ophiocordyceps unilateralis fungus” is commonly known as the zombie-ant fungus (Wikipedia, 2020). This fungus uses ants as hosts typically is typically found in tropical forests (Lu, 2019). It infects the ant by penetrating the exoskeleton and slowly growing inside the ant and taking control over its behavior. Once in full control, the fungus moves the ant into a warm and humid location near the ground, ideal for distributing its spores (Petruzzello, 2023). The fungus will cause the ant to climb up a nearby twig or stem and lock onto it with its jaws (Science Section, Library of Congress). Soon after death follows, the fungus finishes digesting the cadaver before its final stage. For the final stage of the fungus cycle, it erects a stalk from the base of the ant's head that will release spores to infect other ants (Science Section, Library of Congress).

The fungus does not directly control the ant's brain instead, it uses bioactive compounds that interact directly with the ant’s nervous system and directly control the ant's muscles (Lu, 2019). The fungus also differs from typical zombie lore as it does not typically infect an entire colony to keep the ecosystem balanced; the fungi only infect a few ants at any given time (Lu, 2019).

The fungi also seem to have a good level of control over where the ants die, as graveyards of dozens of ants infected with Ophiocordyceps unilateralis have been found together. This suggests just how precisely the fungus can control the death of the infected (Petruzzello).

At the start of the game, there are 3 options to play as: The Momad, The Streetkid, The Corpo-Rat. (Toms, 2020)

Nomad Lifepath: The momad life path with the polar opposite of Corpo. It starts with the player, V, starting in the Badlands outside of Night City. This path allows you to learn about the clans that roam the far reaches of the Cuberpunk 2077. At the start, you will have to make a major life decision: whether to ditch the clan you've been roaming with and drive into Night City. During the drive, your car breaks down and you will have to enlist the age the help of a familiar face to help repair your car (Toms, 2020).

Street Kid: As a street Kid, you will get placed again in Night City from the start, but this time you will start on the ground rather than in one of the corpo-controlled skyscrapers. Corpo: If you pick the Corpo life path, you start at the highest echelons of Night City from the moment you start. You work for Arasaka, working under a man called Arthur Jenkins. Your first quest involves helping Jenkins take down a rival inside the corporation.

The series is set decades after the collapse of society, which was caused by a mass fungal infection that transforms its host into zombie-like creatures (Wikipedia Contributors, 2021). The storyline of The Last of Us follows the character Joel, who is a hardened survivor, and is hired to smuggle a 14-year-old girl called Ellie out of an oppressive quarantine zone. This started off as a small job but soon became a brutal and heartbreaking journey across the U.S. They both depend on each other for survival (IMDB, 2023). The Last of US (Video Game) The Last of Us is a game developed by Naughty Dog Studios and was released on the 14 of June 2013, for the PlayStation 3 (Wikipedia Contributors, 2019b). The Last of Us is a 3rd person adventure survival horror game, as the player you plays as the character Joel (The Last of Us' - Story Trailer, 2013). The game is set in a post-apocalyptic United States where you have to work across the country to find a cure for the modern fungal plague that has decimated most of the human race (The Last of Us' - Story Trailer, 2013). The sequel, The Last of Us Part 2, was released in 2020. The game focuses on Ellie seeking revenge for Joel’s murder and another character, Abby, who is a soldier who has become involved in a conflict between her militia and a rival cult (Wikipedia Contributors, 2020c). The series has won over 500 game of the year awards since 2013 (Sony PlayStation, n.d.).

Naughty Dog began work on The Last of Us Part 1 back in 2009 after the release of Uncharted 2. This was the first time they had split their team into two separate teams, one which worked on Uncharted 3 and the other which worked to develop The Last of US. The game director for this project was Bruce Straley, and the creative director was Neil Druckmann (Wikipedia Contributors, 2019b). Naughty Dog started work on The Last of Us Part 2 in 2014, soon after the release of The Last of Us Part 1. The entire team at Naughty Dog (around 350) moved over to help develop The Last of Us Part 2 (Wikipedia Contributors, 2020c).
