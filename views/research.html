Ophiocordyceps unilateralis fungus: The fungus “Ophiocordyceps unilateralis fungus” is commonly known as the zombie-ant fungus (Wikipedia, 2020). This fungus uses ants as hosts typically is typically found in tropical forests (Lu, 2019). It infects the ant by penetrating the exoskeleton and slowly growing inside the ant and taking control over its behavior. Once in full control, the fungus moves the ant into a warm and humid location near the ground, ideal for distributing its spores (Petruzzello, 2023). The fungus will cause the ant to climb up a nearby twig or stem and lock onto it with its jaws (Science Section, Library of Congress). Soon after death follows, the fungus finishes digesting the cadaver before its final stage. For the final stage of the fungus cycle, it erects a stalk from the base of the ant's head that will release spores to infect other ants (Science Section, Library of Congress).

The fungus does not directly control the ants brain instead it uses bioactive compounds that interact directly with the ant’s nervous system and directly control the ants muscles, (Lu, 2019). The fungus also differs from typical zombie lore as it does not typically infect and entire colony to keep the ecosystem balanced the fungi only infect a few ants at any given time, (Lu, 2019).

The fungi also seems to have a good level of control on where the anys dye as graveyards of dozens of ants infected with ophiocordyceps unilateralis have been found together. This suggest just how precisely the fungus can control the death of the infected, (Petruzzello).
